# Longentis Brand Voice

## Tone

**Clinical. Premium. Direct.**

Write like a trusted physician who respects your time — not a marketing department. Every word earns its place.

## Rules

1. **No corporate speak.** Ban these words entirely: *seamless, robust, cutting-edge, innovative, leverage, synergy, holistic, empower, revolutionize, game-changer, best-in-class, world-class, next-generation*.
2. **No generic adjectives.** Ban: *amazing, incredible, fantastic, awesome, great, wonderful, excellent*. If you can't quantify it, don't say it.
3. **Specificity over superlatives.** "10,000+ patients since 2015" beats "trusted by thousands." Numbers and credentials replace fluff.
4. **Clinical confidence.** Use precise medical terminology where appropriate. The audience is intelligent — don't dumb it down, but don't obscure meaning either.
5. **Respect the reader.** Address him as a peer making an informed decision, not a target being sold to.
6. **Short sentences win.** If a sentence has a comma, ask if it needs one. If a paragraph exceeds three sentences, split it.

## Headline Formula

- Lead with the benefit or identity, not the feature.
- ✅ "Private wellness, for the discerning gent."
- ❌ "The best telehealth platform for men's health solutions."

## CTA Language

- Use direct, low-friction phrasing.
- ✅ "Start your free visit" / "See your options" / "Get your labs"
- ❌ "Sign up now!" / "Join today!" / "Don't miss out!"

## Describing Treatments

- Name the compound, not the category. "Testosterone cypionate" not "hormone therapy."
- State the mechanism briefly. "Blocks PDE5 to restore blood flow" not "helps with ED."
- Include the monitoring angle. "Quarterly labs included" reinforces clinical rigor.

## Audience

The *discerning gent*: 35–65, health-conscious, values privacy, expects premium service, skeptical of hype. He Googles before he buys. He reads the fine print. Earn his trust with evidence, not enthusiasm.
